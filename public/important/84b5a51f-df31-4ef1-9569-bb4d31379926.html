Important Points - Notes

Page 1
  1. Introduction to Poisson distribution applications, covering various real-world scenarios.
  2. Problems involve calculating specific probabilities like 'exactly 2 accidents' or '5 emissions'.
  3. One problem includes determining probability of a guarantee violation based on defective rates.
  4. Poisson distribution applies to rare, independent events occurring at a constant average rate.
  5. Key conditions include number of trials tending to infinity and success probability tending to zero.
  6. The Poisson distribution's mean and variance are both equal to its parameter λ.
  7. The formula P(X=x) = (λ^x * e^-λ) / x! is used for calculating probabilities.
Page 2
  1. Further Poisson distribution problems for demand, errors, and defective items.
  2. Calculate probabilities for no demand, refused demand, and varying error counts.
  3. Problems also involve approximating numbers of items with defects in large consignments.
  4. One scenario requires calculating bacterial growth probability across multiple test tubes.
  5. Several exercises focus on fitting a Poisson distribution to given observed frequency data.
  6. These problems reinforce the application of Poisson distribution in diverse practical contexts.
Page 3
  1. Continued practice with fitting Poisson distributions to empirical frequency data.
  2. Introduction to Geometric Distribution, focusing on the first success in a sequence of trials.
  3. Problems cover calculating the probability of the first success at a specific trial number.
  4. Scenarios include sales calls, dart throws, and finding a specific type of person.
  5. Determine expected number of trials and variance until the first success.
  6. One problem involves expected spending in a game until the first win.
Page 4
  1. Geometric distribution conditions: two outcomes, constant success probability, P(X=x)=p*q^(x-1).
  2. Mean (1/p) and variance ((1-p)/p^2) are key parameters for geometric distribution.
  3. Problems include calculating success probabilities within a number of tries and expected attempts.
  4. Introduction to Continuous Uniform Distribution (U[a,b]), where all outcomes are equally likely.
  5. Problems involve finding probabilities for intervals, mean, and variance of uniform distributions.
  6. Applications include bus breakdowns, conference durations, and randomly chosen points.
  7. The uniform distribution's mean is (b+a)/2 and variance is (b-a)^2/12.
Page 5
  1. Uniform distribution concepts: PDF is constant within interval, CDF is (x-a)/(b-a).
  2. Problems calculate probabilities for coffee volume, train arrival times, and waiting times.
  3. One problem involves a conditional probability for dental charges exceeding a certain amount.
  4. Introduction to Exponential Distribution, modeling time between events or system lifetimes.
  5. Problems calculate probabilities for shower duration, telephone calls, and bus arrival times.
  6. Applications include finding probabilities for net profit exceeding a threshold over days.
Page 6
  1. Further exponential distribution problems, calculating probabilities for profit and sales tax.
  2. Exponential distribution PDF: f(x) = αe^(-αx) and parameters: Mean = 1/α, Variance = 1/α^2.
  3. Problems cover compressor life, computer response times, and tyre mileage analysis.
  4. Introduction to Normal (Gaussian) Distribution for bulb life and cholesterol values.
  5. Problems involve estimating numbers or probabilities based on mean and standard deviation.
  6. One problem focuses on finding normal distribution parameters from given percentile data.
Page 7
  1. Normal distribution applications in examinations, heights, and product quality control.
  2. Problems include estimating average marks, passing scores, and student counts within height ranges.
  3. The normal distribution PDF and parameters (mean μ, variance σ^2) are introduced.
  4. Calculate defective percentages for manufactured parts given tolerance limits.
  5. Estimate product replacement numbers or income distribution from normal data.
  6. These problems demonstrate the wide applicability of the normal distribution in statistics.
Page 8
  1. Final normal distribution problem determining minimum marks for specific grades.
  2. Introduction to Gamma Distribution, modeling power consumption, computer life, and survival times.
  3. Gamma distribution PDF and parameters: Mean = αβ, Variance = αβ^2.
  4. Problems calculate probabilities for given time intervals using the gamma distribution.
  5. Determine gamma distribution parameters from given mean and variance (e.g., insurance claims).
  6. Applications include probability of insufficient stock given gamma distributed consumption.
